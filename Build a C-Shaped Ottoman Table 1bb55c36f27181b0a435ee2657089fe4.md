# Build a C-Shaped Ottoman Table

<aside>

### **Materials List**

- **Hardwood Boards**: 1" thick; enough to cut for a tabletop of 40.5" x 20" and two sides each 18" high x 20" wide.
- **3/8" Dowels**: Hardwood.
- **Wood Glue**: High-quality such as Titebond III.
- **Finish**: Polyurethane or your preferred wood finish.
- **Casters or Wheels**: Suitable for smooth sliding.
- **Track or Rail System**: Two tracks, each 40-50" long, depending on availability.
- **Sandpaper**: Various grits for palm sander.
- **Felt Pads** (optional): To protect the ottoman and floor.
</aside>

<aside>

### **Tools Needed**

- **8” Table Saw**
- **Palm Sander**
- **Router** (optional, for edges)
- **Nail Gun** (optional, for additional hold or assembly)
- **Bar Clamps**
- **Dowel Jig**: Purchase from Harbor Freight.
- **Drill with Drill Bits and Stop Collar**
- **Measuring Tape and Pencil**
- **Rubber Mallet**
</aside>

<aside>

### **Construction Steps**

- **Day 1: Planning and Material Acquisition (2-3 Hours)**
    1. **Design Review**: Double-check the design and dimensions.
    2. **Purchase Materials**: Source all materials including dowels, wood, and track system.
- **Day 2: Cutting Wood to Size (2-3 Hours)**
    1. **Cut Tabletop and Sides**: Using the table saw, cut the hardwood boards to the following sizes:
        - **Tabletop**: 40.5" length x 20" width.
        - **Sides**: 18" height x 20" width (2 pieces).
    2. **Router Edges** (Optional): Use a router to round off or bevel the edges for a finished look.
- **Day 3: Marking and Drilling for Dowels (2-3 Hours)**
    1. **Mark Dowel Positions**: On both the tabletop and side panels, mark where dowels will go. Ensure alignment for a tight fit.
    2. **Drilling Holes**:
        - Set up the dowel jig according to instructions.
        - Drill dowel holes using the drill with a stop collar to ensure uniform depth.
- **Day 4: Dry Fitting and Gluing (2-3 Hours)**
    1. **Dry Fit**: Assemble the tabletop and sides without glue to ensure a perfect fit.
    2. **Glue and Assemble**:
        - Apply wood glue in the dowel holes and on the dowels.
        - Assemble the pieces, using a rubber mallet to ensure dowels fit snugly.
        - Clamp the assembly to dry.
- **Day 5: Sanding and Applying Finish (2-3 Hours)**
    1. **Sanding**:
        - Sand the entire assembly starting with a coarse grit and moving to a fine grit.
    2. **Apply First Coat of Finish**:
        - Clean the wood of all dust.
        - Apply the first coat of finish and allow it to dry.
- **Day 6: Final Finish Application (2-3 Hours)**
    1. **Light Sanding**:
        - Lightly sand between coats if necessary.
    2. **Apply Final Coat(s)**:
        - Apply the second and possibly third coats of finish, allowing adequate drying time between coats.
- **Day 7: Installing Track and Casters (2-3 Hours)**
    1. **Install Casters/Wheels**:
        - Attach casters or wheels to the bottom of the side panels.
    2. **Track System Setup**:
        - Install the track or rail system on the floor where the table will slide.
        - Ensure tracks are parallel and securely mounted.
</aside>

<aside>

### **Additional Recommendations**

- **Test Sliding Mechanism**: Before finalizing, test the sliding mechanism to ensure smooth operation.
- **Adjustments**: Make any necessary adjustments to ensure the table slides easily and aligns correctly.
