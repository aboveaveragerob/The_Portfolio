### **Materials & Tools Checklist**

- **Materials**
    - **2x4s**: Two for the top and bottom plates, and additional 2x4s for vertical studs (number depends on wall length).
    - **Lateral Bracing**: Optional 2x4 for added stability.
    - **Drywall**: One ½-inch sheet (if finishing the wall).
    - **Screws**:
    - **Drywall Screws**: 1 ¼-inch for attaching drywall.
    - **Wood Screws**: 3-inch for framing.
    - **Masonry Screws**: Tapcons, if anchoring to a concrete floor.
    - **Wood Shims**: For leveling the bottom plate.
- **Tools**
    - **Drill + Bits**: Both wood and masonry bits.
    - **Level**: At least 24 inches long.
    - **Measuring Tape & Pencil**.
    - **Speed Square**.
    - **Circular Saw or Handsaw**.
    - **Stud Finder**.
    - **Utility Knife**: For cutting drywall.
</aside>

### **Step-by-Step Building Guide**

- **Day 1: Planning & Layout (1-2 Hours)**
    1. **Measure and Mark the Wall Location**:
        - Use a chalk line or pencil to precisely mark where the wall will be installed on the floor, ensuring alignment with existing walls using a speed square.
    2. **Mark Stud Placement on Bottom & Top Plates**:
        - Layout the stud placement, marking 16 inches on center on both top and bottom 2x4s for standard stud spacing.
- **Day 2: Framing the Half-Wall (2-3 Hours)**
    - **Cut the Lumber**:
        - Cut two 2x4s to the length of your wall for the top and bottom plates.
        - Cut vertical studs to your desired wall height (typically between 36 to 48 inches).
    - **Assemble the Frame**:
        - Lay out the top and bottom plates on a flat surface.
        - Align vertical studs with your pre-marked points.
        - Use 3-inch wood screws or nails to secure the studs to the plates, employing toe-screwing for added stability.
- **Day 3: Installing the Wall Frame (2-3 Hours)**
    - **Secure the Bottom Plate**:
        - For concrete floors, drill pilot holes and secure with Tapcon screws every 24 inches.
        - For wood subfloors, use 3-inch wood screws every 16 to 24 inches.
        - Use shims as necessary to ensure the bottom plate is perfectly level.
    - **Secure the Top of the Wall**:
        - Attach to an adjacent wall using L-brackets for additional support, if not freestanding.
- **Day 4: Stability Bracing (2-3 Hours)**
    - **Choose a Bracing Method**:
        1. **Anchor to an Adjacent Wall**: Secure the end stud to the nearest existing wall using L-brackets.
        2. **Diagonal Bracing**: Install diagonal 2x4 braces cut at 45° angles within the frame.
        3. **Ceiling Post**: Attach a vertical 2x4 from the top of the half-wall to the ceiling for maximum stability.
- **Day 5: Drywall & Finishing (3-4 Hours)**
    - **Install Drywall**:
        - Measure and cut ½-inch drywall to fit the frame.
        - Attach it using 1¼-inch drywall screws spaced every 12 inches along the studs.
    - **Tape, Mud, and Sand**:
        - Apply joint compound to all seams, allow to dry, and sand smooth. Repeat for 2-3 coats until seamless.
- **Day 6: Paint & Trim (2-3 Hours)**
    - **Finishing Touches**:
        - Prime and paint the drywall.
        - Install baseboard trim to match the existing room décor.
</aside>

<aside>

### **Final Adjustments**

- **Check Stability**: If the wall feels wobbly, consider adding additional L-brackets or bracing.
- **Optional Wood Top**: Instead of drywall, consider capping the wall with a 1x6 or 2x6 wood plank for a different aesthetic.
