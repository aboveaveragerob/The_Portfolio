# Build a Non-Load Bearing Wall

Bailiwicks: Woodworking (https://app.notion.com/p/Woodworking-2a355c36f271802e9965de950cb5cead?pvs=21)
Reading Category: Guide or How-To

<aside>

<aside>

### **Project Overview**

- **Wall Dimensions:** 80” tall x 90” long**Anchors:** Ceiling-mounted and VHB tape on the floor
- **Bracing:** L-brackets at the existing wall and diagonal bracing inside the frame
- **Finish:** Drywall on both sides with base molding
</aside>

<aside>

### **Day 1: Planning & Prep (1-2 Hours)**

- **Gather Materials & Tools**: Collect all necessary wood, fasteners, tools, and safety gear.
- **Locate Ceiling Joists**: Use a stud finder to mark where the top plate will attach.
- **Mark Wall Layout**: Use a chalk line to mark the position on the floor and ceiling, ensuring everything is square using a speed square.
</aside>

<aside>

### **Day 2: Installing the Ceiling Plate (1-2 Hours)**

- **Cut and Position the Ceiling Plate**: Cut a 2x4 to 90 inches and position it against the ceiling where previously marked.
- **Secure with Shims and Screws**: Check for level, shim as necessary, and secure with 3” screws into the ceiling joists.
</aside>

<aside>

### **Day 3: Installing the Floor Plate (1-2 Hours)**

- **Prepare the Floor Surface**: Clean thoroughly with isopropyl alcohol and allow to dry.
- **Apply VHB Tape and Set Plate**: Attach VHB tape to the bottom of the floor plate, position it directly under the ceiling plate, and press firmly to bond.
</aside>

<aside>

### **Day 4: Cutting & Installing Wall Studs (2 Hours)**

- **Measure and Cut Studs**: Measure the height between the top and bottom plates, cut studs to fit slightly shorter to allow easy installation.
- **Install Studs**: Start from one end and work to the other, securing studs to the plates using angled screws or pocket hole screws.
</aside>

<aside>

### **Day 5: Installing Bracing for Stability (1-2 Hours)**

- **Attach L-Brackets**: Secure the partition wall to the existing wall with L-brackets at the top, middle, and near the bottom.
- **Install Diagonal Bracing**: Cut 2x4s at a 45-degree angle and install between studs for added stability.
</aside>

<aside>

### **Day 6: Drywall Installation (Side 1) (2 Hours)**

- **Measure, Cut, and Attach Drywall**: Cut drywall to fit and screw into place on one side of the wall, ensuring screws enter studs and are spaced every 12 inches.
</aside>

<aside>

### **Day 7: Drywall Installation (Side 2) (2 Hours)**

- **Repeat Drywall Installation**: Install drywall on the other side of the wall, following the same process as Day 6.
</aside>

<aside>

### **Day 8: Finishing & Sanding (2 Hours)**

- **Apply Joint Compound**: Cover all seams and screw holes with joint compound, smooth with a putty knife, and allow to dry.
- **Sand Smooth**: Once dry, sand the joint compound smooth, preparing for a second coat if necessary.
</aside>

<aside>

### **Day 9: Final Sanding & Painting Prep (1-2 Hours)**

- **Second Coat of Joint Compound**: Apply a second layer of joint compound if needed, let dry, and perform a final sanding.
- **Prep for Painting**: Ensure the wall is clean and dust-free.
</aside>

<aside>

### **Day 10: Painting & Molding (2 Hours)**

- **Prime and Paint**: Apply primer if desired, then paint the wall in your chosen color
- **Install Base Molding**: Measure, cut, and attach base molding to the bottom of the wall for a finished look.
</aside>
