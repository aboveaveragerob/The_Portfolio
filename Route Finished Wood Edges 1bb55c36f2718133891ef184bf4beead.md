# Route Finished Wood Edges

### **Tools Needed**

- **Router**: A handheld router is perfect for this task.
- **Router Bits**:
    - **Rounding Bit** (also known as a roundover bit) for rounded edges.
    - **Bevel Bit** for beveled edges.
- **Clamps**: To secure the wood while routing.
- **Safety Gear**: Safety glasses and hearing protection.
</aside>

<aside>

### **Step-by-Step Guide**

After cutting your pieces to size:

- **Step 1: Setup**
    - **Select Your Bit**
        - **For Rounded Edges**: Attach a roundover bit to your router. This bit will have a curved profile.
        - **For Beveled Edges**: Attach a bevel bit to your router. This bit will have an angled profile.
    - **Adjust Router Depth**
        - Set the depth of the cut according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Generally, you want the depth to match the radius of the curve or the angle depth for bevels.
- **Step 2: Secure Your Wood**
    1. **Clamp Down Your Workpiece**:
        - Firmly clamp the wood to your workbench to prevent it from moving. Ensure there's enough space around the edges for the router to move freely.
- **Step 3: Routing the Edges**
    1. **Perform a Test Run (Optional but Recommended)**:
        - If you have scrap wood of the same type and thickness, perform a test run to check the depth and feel of the router.
    2. **Route the Edges**:
        - Turn on the router and let it reach full speed before making contact with the wood.
        - **For Rounded Edges**: Move the router along the edges of the wood in a steady, continuous motion, keeping the router base flat against the surface of the wood.
        - **For Beveled Edges**: Similar to rounded edges, maintain a steady pace and even pressure.
        - **Important**: Always move the router in the opposite direction of the spinning bit (left to right or counter-clockwise around the piece) to ensure control and prevent the bit from digging too deep.
    3. **Flip and Repeat**:
        - Flip the wood to the opposite side and repeat the process to ensure all visible edges are finished.
- **Step 4: Sanding**
    1. **Light Sanding**:
        - After routing, there might be minor burrs or roughness. Use a fine-grit sandpaper to lightly sand the routed edges for a smooth finish.
- **Step 5: Clean Up**
    1. **Vacuum or Wipe Down**:
        - Clean up any sawdust or debris from your work area and the wood surface to prepare for the next steps.
